Early Life and Career Beginnings

Jonathan Van Ness was born in Quincy, Illinois, and developed an interest in hairstyling from a young age. He attended the Aveda Institute in Minneapolis, where he honed his skills before moving to Los Angeles. In LA, he started working at the Sally Hershberger Salon, which marked the beginning of his professional career.

Rise to Fame

Van Ness first gained public attention with his web series “Gay of Thrones,” a parody of the popular HBO series “Game of Thrones.” His unique and humorous commentary earned him an Emmy nomination in 2018. However, it was his role in the Netflix series “Queer Eye” that catapulted him to international fame. As the grooming expert on the show, Van Ness won the hearts of viewers with his vibrant personality and genuine empathy.

Authorial Success

In addition to his television career, Van Ness has also found success as an author. His memoir, “Over the Top: A Raw Journey to Self-Love,” was published in 2019 and quickly became a New York Times Best Seller. The book details his struggles with addiction, body image, and sexuality, offering readers an intimate look into his life.

Podcast and Endorsements

Van Ness hosts a popular podcast, “Getting Curious with Jonathan Van Ness,” where he explores various topics that pique his interest. He has also landed several endorsement deals with brands like Essie, Biossance, and Smirnoff, which contribute significantly to his income.

Public Speaking and Advocacy

As an openly gay man living with HIV, Van Ness uses his platform to advocate for LGBTQ+ rights and HIV awareness. He is a sought-after public speaker, earning substantial fees for his appearances at events and conferences.

Personal Life

Van Ness is currently based in Austin, Texas, and is married to Mark Peacock. He is known for his love of gymnastics and figure skating, often sharing videos of his routines on social media.

Real Estate Investments
