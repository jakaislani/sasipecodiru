She has been named AP Sports Columnist of the Year five times and received the National Press Foundation’s 2017 Chairman’s Citation. Further, she was also nominated for the 2020 Pulitzer Prize.

Sally has also written a dozen books. She is well-known for her work on Pat Summitt, Joe Paterno, Lance Armstrong, and the US Center for SafeSport.

Early Life, Family, and Education

Sally Jenkins grew up in Fort Worth, Texas, and was born on October 22, 1960.

Her dad, Dan Jenkins, was a famous sportswriter and writer for Sports Illustrated. He was really good at golf, too, playing it during his college days at Texas Christian University.

Dan was born in Fort Worth on December 2, 1928, and sadly passed away on March 7, 2019. Likewise, Sally’s mother’s name is June Jenkins. 

She also has two brothers, Marty and Danny Jenkins. 

Sally went to Stanford University and graduated in 1982 with a degree in English literature. She then followed in her dad’s footsteps and became a sports columnist for The Washington Post.

Career

Sally Jenkins is a sports columnist and writer for The Washington Post. She used to work as a senior writer for Sports Illustrated. She has written twelve books, four of which became New York Times bestsellers.

One of her most famous books is “It’s Not About the Bike,” written by cyclist Lance Armstrong, with Sally Jenkins, which became a number-one bestseller.

Jenkins has also written for Smithsonian Magazine, GQ, and Sports Illustrated and has appeared as a correspondent on CNBC and NPR’s All Things Considered.

During her career, Jenkins conducted a notable interview with football coach Joe Paterno of Penn State University before his death.

She reported his words regarding the Jerry Sandusky sexual molestation allegations without drawing firm conclusions about his involvement.

However, after the release of the Freeh Report, she wrote a column stating that Paterno had lied and fallen victim to the corrosive belief that winning makes one better than others.

Awards & Achievements 

Jenkins has received numerous awards and accolades throughout her career. Here is a list of some of the notable awards she has won:

  • William Hill Sports Book of the Year (2000)
  • New York Times Best Seller List (2000)
  • Christopher Award for Adult Books (2001)
  • Texas Tayshas Reading List (2001-2002)
  • Associated Press’s Sports Columnist of the Year Award (2001, 2003, 2010, 2011, 2021)
  • Sports Columnist of the Year by the Society of Professional Journalists (2001, 2008, 2011)
  • National Press Foundation’s Chairman Citation (2017)
  • National Sportscasters and Sportswriters Hall of Fame (2005)
  • Washington DC Sports Hall of Fame (2021)
  • Red Smith Award (2021)

Interesting Facts

  • In October 2022, Sally criticized the United States Center for SafeSport in a Washington Post column. She called it a “false front” and accused it of being a cover-up operation and failing to take action on complaints.
  • She co-wrote autobiographies with cyclist Lance Armstrong and defended him even after he admitted to doping and taking banned performance-enhancing substances.
